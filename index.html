🍾 Yeast Pitch Rate Guide
▼

How much yeast to use, why it matters, and how rehydration makes a bigger difference than amount.

What Determines How Much Yeast You Need

Two things determine your pitch rate: batch volume and starting gravity. Honey amount only matters because it affects gravity. Higher gravity means more sugar stress on yeast — you need more viable cells to start fermentation cleanly.

Basic Rule

For dry yeast used in mead: ~1g yeast per gallon is the standard starting point. Most yeast packets are 5g — designed for 5 gallons.

Why Underpitching Causes Problems

Too little yeast means slower fermentation, stressed yeast cells, more fusel alcohols, and sulfur smells. Most noticeable when SG > 1.090, nutrients are low, or temperature is high.

· Slower fermentation start
· Stressed, struggling yeast
· More fusel alcohols (harsh flavour)
· Sulfur smells
Rehydration Makes a Bigger Difference Than Amount

Using GoFerm during rehydration increases viable yeast cells dramatically — especially important for high gravity meads. Proper rehydration often matters more than pitching extra yeast.

Rehydration Process
1. Heat water to 40°C (104°F)
2. Add GoFerm and stir
3. Add yeast
4. Wait 15 minutes
5. Add to must
🧪 Nutrient Science Reference
▼

What the Nutrient Advisor is based on — the science behind scheduling decisions and nutrient type differences.

The Two Types of Nitrogen

All nutrients fall into two categories that behave very differently in fermentation.

Type
What it is
Organic (YAN)
Amino acids from dead yeast — slow release, hard to overdose
Inorganic (DAP)
Diammonium phosphate — fast release, easy to overdose
Fermaid-O — Organic Nutrient

Slow release nitrogen, hard to overdose, much safer for small batches. The preferred choice for mead — reduces fusel alcohols, improves aroma, reduces sulfur. This is why modern meadmakers follow TOSNA (Tailored Organic Staggered Nutrient Addition) using only Fermaid-O.

Typical amount: 3–5g per gallon

DAP — Inorganic Nutrient

Very fast nitrogen source — yeast absorb it immediately. Easy to overdose and can cause off-flavours if dumped in all at once. DAP should almost always be staggered. Do not add after ~9–10% ABV — yeast stop using it efficiently.

Typical amount: ~1g per gallon

Batch size does NOT change the strategy — only gravity matters. A 1 gallon and 5 gallon batch at the same gravity stress yeast identically. Nutrient needs scale linearly with volume.

GoFerm — Yeast Rehydration Nutrient

GoFerm is not a fermentation nutrient. It is used only during yeast rehydration before pitching — never added directly to must.

Purpose
· Protects yeast cell membranes during rehydration
· Increases yeast survival rate
· Improves fermentation start
⚠️ Never add GoFerm directly to must. It is dissolved in warm water first, then yeast is rehydrated in that solution before pitching. Adding it to must defeats the purpose and can harm fermentation.

Your recipes and batches are saved on your device only — not in the cloud. This means if you clear your browser, reinstall the app, or get a new phone, your data could be lost.

The Backup tab lets you save everything to a file and bring it back whenever you need it. Think of it like saving your game.

📦 Backing Up Your Data
1Tap Export Full Backup below.
2A file called meadery_backup_[date].json will download to your device.
3Save that file somewhere safe — Google Drive, your camera roll, email it to yourself, anything works.
4Do this before updating the app, switching phones, or sharing your setup with someone.
📥 Restoring From a Backup
1Tap Choose Backup File below.
2Find and select your meadery_backup_[date].json file.
3The app will merge your backup in — adding anything that wasn't already there without deleting what you have.
4Check your Recipes and Batches tabs — everything should be back exactly as you left it.
📤 Sharing a Single Recipe With a Friend
